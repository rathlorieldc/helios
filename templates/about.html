About Helios


What it Does


Helios uses climate change projection data about various climate factors to help you choose the best possible place for retirement. The projections are based on two scenarios: humans changing course to significantly lower emissions, or humans continuing on the current course to increasingly higher emissions.

How it Works


To use the program, choose a location and the decade you plan to retire and we will search our database for climate change projections so you can make an informed decision about where to retire. We did our best to come up with a comprehensive list of locations for the continental United States. However, if you do not see your desired destination on list, pick the closest known location and the results will be very similar, if not the same.

About the Data


Climate data is organized by county and natural disasters data is organized by state. We felt the most relevant climate factors for a comfortable retirement destination were average temperature, temperature extremes, total precipitation, and number of dry days a year. Our retirement score is an average of the combined scores for each climate variable, which are calculated using formulas that assess deviation from either an ideal value or the national average, depending on the variable.
